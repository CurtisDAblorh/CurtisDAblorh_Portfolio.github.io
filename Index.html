My Projects.

3D-Printed Orthotic Exoskeleton

Designed and coded with C++ to rehabilitate patients with muscular dystrophy. This was able to be calibrated by the patient using touch sensitive pins that would read the magnitude of an EMG signal picked up by contracting their forearms. The exosleton would then read the contraction and location, process this information and move the fingers using the linear actuator motors.

Autonomous Obstacle Avoidance wheelchair

Here I created a C++ algorithm to read the input from multiple ultra sonic sensors, combine this and direct the prototype around any obstacle without the patient having to lift a finger. The patient could decide the overall direction the wheelchair would move in but the obstacle avoidance algorithm would take control when something was too close to the mooving wheelchair. This was a huge success and ended up winning me 1st place in the Rapid electronics contest! Coded a Bluetooth compatible mobile app (Java) which alerted the user with infographics and the appropriate sounds when a nearby obstacle was detected and the obstacle avoidance system triggered.
